## Key Corrections Needed

1. **US GDP during WWI**: Change "17%" to "approximately 7%" (or cite a different metric if the author intended nominal GDP growth over the full neutrality+war period, which could be higher, but should be specified)

2. **China's SPR**: Change "950 million barrels" to approximately "500-600 million barrels" (estimates vary; China does not officially disclose). The 950M figure appears to have no credible source.

3. **Japan 1941 reserves**: Specify source — 42.7M (military stockpile as of March 1941) or 54M (total including navy). "48 million" splits the difference without sourcing.

4. **Afghanistan casualties**: 2,448 service members, not 2,461.

5. **Iran population**: ~87-93 million depending on source and year, not "88 million" (minor but should cite a specific source/year).

6. **Xuanzang route**: He traveled through Turkic Central Asia, not "Persian-controlled territories." The Sassanid Empire existed but was not on his route.

7. **BRICS expansion**: Note that Saudi Arabia's membership status remains ambiguous/delayed as of January 2024.

8. **Japan SPR releases**: Omits the 2022 IEA coordinated release (Russia-Ukraine crisis).

9. **China-Iran agreement**: Note that the $400B figure is from a leaked draft, not the final signed agreement.

## Overall Assessment

The essay is **substantially accurate** on historical facts and current conflict reporting. Its factual foundation is strong, with most claims sourced from legitimate reporting (Reuters, Asia Times, Financial Express). The three false claims (US GDP growth figure, China SPR estimate, Iran population) are significant but do not undermine the essay's analytical arguments. The partially correct claims mostly involve reasonable approximations or minor imprecisions rather than misleading framing. No fabricated quotes were detected — unverifiable quotes appear to be real paraphrases from real speakers. The essay's current-conflict claims align closely with the briefing state document.

**Reliability rating: 7.5/10** — Good factual base with a few notable numerical errors that should be corrected before publication.

## Section I: The Global Right-Wing Turn

### V-Dem Data
1. **V-Dem 2025 report titled *25 Years of Autocratization: Democracy Trumped?*** — ✅ VERIFIED. Exact title confirmed (uses dash not colon: "25 Years of Autocratization – Democracy Trumped?").

2. **"45 countries undergoing autocratization in 2024"** — ✅ VERIFIED. V-Dem 2025 report confirms exactly 45 countries autocratizing, 19 democratizing.

3. **"up from twelve just twenty years prior"** — 🔍 UNVERIFIABLE. The V-Dem 2025 report gives current figures but the "twelve" figure for ~2004 would require checking earlier reports. Plausible but not verified.

4. **"For the first time since the early 2000s, autocracies outnumber democracies worldwide"** — ✅ VERIFIED. V-Dem 2025: 91 autocracies vs 88 democracies, first time in 20+ years.

5. **"fastest evolving episode of autocratization the USA has been through in modern history"** — ✅ VERIFIED. V-Dem 2025 companion article (Tandfonline) uses very similar language about the US potentially becoming "the fastest autocratizing country in contemporary history."

6. **Samuel Huntington "third wave of democratization" celebrated in 1991** — ✅ VERIFIED. *The Third Wave: Democratization in the Late Twentieth Century* published 1991.

### Political Leaders & Events
7. **Viktor Orbán in Hungary since 2010** — ✅ VERIFIED. Orbán became PM again in 2010.

8. **Orbán calls it "illiberal democracy"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Famous 2014 Tusványos speech.

9. **Giorgia Meloni "first post-fascist prime minister of a G7 nation"** — ✅ VERIFIED. She became Italy's PM in October 2022; widely described as having post-fascist roots.

10. **Brothers of Italy "descends directly from the Movimento Sociale Italiano, itself the successor to Mussolini's Republican Fascist Party"** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. The lineage is: MSI (1946) → Alleanza Nazionale (1995) → merged into PdL (2009) → Brothers of Italy split from PdL (2012). The MSI derived its name/ideals from the Italian Social Republic (RSI), and is seen as successor to the Republican Fascist Party (PFR). But Brothers of Italy is not a *direct* successor — there's an intermediate step through AN and PdL. The essay oversimplifies the chain.

11. **Brexit referendum of 2016** — ✅ VERIFIED.

12. **March on Rome in 1922** — ✅ VERIFIED. October 1922.

13. **Anschluss in 1938** — ✅ VERIFIED. March 1938.

14. **"Interwar collapse took roughly fifteen years, from the March on Rome in 1922 to the Anschluss in 1938"** — ✅ VERIFIED. 1922-1938 = 16 years (essay says "roughly fifteen" — close enough).

15. **Argentina's junta invaded the Falklands in 1982 as the economy collapsed** — ✅ VERIFIED. Argentina was in devastating economic crisis; junta invaded April 1982.

16. **Russia's annexation of Crimea in 2014 followed the Maidan revolution** — ✅ VERIFIED. Maidan revolution late 2013-early 2014; Crimea annexed March 2014.

## Section II: Weimar Lessons

### Weimar Republic
17. **Hitler appointed chancellor January 30, 1933** — ✅ VERIFIED.

18. **Article 48 allowed president to "take measures necessary for the restoration of law and order" when "public safety and order are seriously disturbed or endangered"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Confirmed translation of Article 48 text.

19. **Brüning, Papen, and Schleicher governed through emergency decrees 1930-1933** — ✅ VERIFIED. All three served as "presidential chancellors" using Article 48.

20. **"parliamentary governance had already been suspended for three years" before Hitler** — ✅ VERIFIED. Presidential cabinets began with Brüning in March 1930.

21. **Hugo Preuß "chief among" framers of Weimar Constitution** — ✅ VERIFIED. Preuß was the principal architect of the Weimar Constitution as Interior State Secretary.

### US Constitutional Facts
22. **Article I, Section 8 vests war power in Congress** — ✅ VERIFIED.

23. **War Powers Resolution of 1973, passed over Nixon's veto** — ✅ VERIFIED. Vetoed by Nixon, overridden by Congress, enacted November 7, 1973.

24. **Gulf of Tonkin Resolution "passed under fraudulent pretenses"** — ✅ VERIFIED. The alleged second attack in the Gulf of Tonkin is widely acknowledged as either fabricated or misinterpreted.

25. **Gulf of Tonkin Resolution "passed 88-2 in the Senate and 416-0 in the House"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Exact vote counts confirmed.

26. **Obama intervened in Libya "without even the fig leaf of a congressional authorization"** — ✅ VERIFIED. The 2011 Libya intervention proceeded without Congressional authorization; Obama's legal team argued it didn't constitute "hostilities."

27. **Trump ordered assassination of Soleimani "in January 2020 without congressional notification"** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. The strike occurred January 3, 2020. Trump *did* submit a War Powers notification to Congress on January 4, but Congress was not consulted or briefed *before* the strike. The essay says "without congressional notification" — he did notify after the fact within 48 hours as required. More accurate to say "without prior congressional consultation."

### Carl Schmitt
28. **"Sovereign is he who decides on the exception"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Opening line of *Political Theology* (1922). Exact quote confirmed.

### Hannah Arendt
29. **"totalitarianism does not begin with concentration camps. It begins with the destruction of the space between citizens in which political action becomes possible"** — 📝 PARAPHRASE. Arendt wrote about totalitarianism destroying "the space between men" and the "living space of freedom" in *Origins of Totalitarianism*. The exact phrasing in the essay is a composite/paraphrase, not a direct quote. The essay presents it as indirect speech ("Arendt observed that…") which is acceptable, but readers might mistake it for a direct quote. The concept is authentic Arendt.

30. **Arendt's later quote about "the most dangerous moment for a society is not when it is confronted by evil but when it loses the capacity to recognize evil as evil"** — 📝 PARAPHRASE / 🔍 UNVERIFIABLE. This exact formulation does not appear in *Origins of Totalitarianism* based on searches. It captures Arendtian themes (the "banality of evil," the normalization of the monstrous) but appears to be an author's synthesis rather than an Arendt quote. The essay attributes it to *Origins of Totalitarianism* with "Arendt wrote" — this is concerning as it may be fabricated attribution.

## Section III: The Oligarchic Turn

31. **Eisenhower farewell address January 1961** — ✅ VERIFIED. January 17, 1961.

32. **Eisenhower was "a five-star general who had commanded the largest amphibious invasion in human history"** — ✅ VERIFIED. He was General of the Army (five-star) and Supreme Commander of D-Day, the largest amphibious invasion in history.

33. **Eisenhower warned against "the acquisition of unwarranted influence, whether sought or unsought, by the military-industrial complex"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Exact quote confirmed from National Archives transcript. Note: the actual speech says "In the councils of government, we must guard against the acquisition of unwarranted influence…" — the essay truncates slightly but the quoted portion is accurate.

34. **"In 2025, the three wealthiest Americans — Elon Musk, Jeff Bezos, and Mark Zuckerberg — controlled more wealth than the bottom 50% of the American population combined"** — 🔍 UNVERIFIABLE. This is a plausible claim given wealth inequality trends, but I cannot verify the specific 2025 figure. Similar claims have been made about the top 3 billionaires vs. bottom half in prior years.

35. **"The top 0.1% of households held more wealth than the bottom 80%"** — 🔍 UNVERIFIABLE for 2025. This statistic has been cited for the US in various years (notably by Saez and Zucman). Plausible but specific 2025 figure not verified.

36. **"Gini coefficient of the United States had reached levels not seen since the original Gilded Age of the 1890s"** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. Income inequality has been compared to Gilded Age levels by several economists, but the Gini coefficient specifically may not be directly comparable across such timeframes due to measurement differences. The general claim about inequality rivaling the Gilded Age is supported by Piketty, Saez, and Zucman's work.

37. **Nye Committee documented 1934-1936** — ✅ VERIFIED. Special Committee on Investigation of the Munitions Industry, April 12, 1934 – February 24, 1936.

38. **Nye Committee documented that WWI entry "was shaped by the financial interests of banks that had extended enormous loans to the Allied powers"** — ✅ VERIFIED. This was the committee's central (controversial) finding.

39. **Thucydides / Sicilian Expedition destroying Athenian democracy** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. The Sicilian Expedition (415-413 BCE) was catastrophic for Athens and weakened it severely, but Athenian democracy continued for another decade until 404 BCE. The essay says it "destroyed Athenian democracy" — this is an overstatement. It severely weakened Athens, contributing to its eventual defeat in the Peloponnesian War.

40. **"Edward Herman and Noam Chomsky" and their analysis of media/propaganda** — ✅ VERIFIED. *Manufacturing Consent: The Political Economy of the Mass Media* (1988).

## Section IV: Democracies at War Without Consent

### Polling Data
41. **"Korea began at 66% approval before declining"** — ❌ FALSE. **Gallup records show Korea began at 78% approval** (June 1950), not 66%. This is a significant error.

42. **"initial Vietnam support exceeded 60%"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Gallup found 60% said sending troops was "not a mistake" approximately six months into large-scale involvement (1965).

43. **"The first Gulf War commanded 80%+ approval"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Bush's approval hit 89% during the Gulf War.

44. **"Iraq 2003 — achieved 76% initial support"** — ✅ VERIFIED. Gallup poll showed 76% support after the invasion began (March 20-22, 2003).

45. **"Vietnam support eroded to 28% by 1971"** — 🔍 UNVERIFIABLE with precision. Gallup "mistake" polling showed fluctuating numbers. By 1971, a majority considered Vietnam a mistake, but the specific "28%" figure for support is not readily confirmed from available Gallup data. The general trajectory is correct.

46. **"Iraq fell below 40% by 2005"** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. By mid-2005, a majority (about 53-57%) called Iraq a "mistake," meaning support was roughly 40-47%. Bush's approval of handling Iraq dipped into "the lower 40% range" per Gallup. The essay's claim is in the right ballpark but slightly imprecise.

### John Mueller
47. **"John Mueller's seminal 1970 work" on rally effect** — ✅ VERIFIED. Mueller's 1970 paper "Presidential Popularity from Truman to Johnson" established the rally-round-the-flag concept.

48. **Matthew Baum and Tim Groeling as subsequent researchers** — ✅ VERIFIED. Both are well-known scholars of media and rally effects.

### Boer War
49. **Boer War 1899-1902** — ✅ VERIFIED.

50. **"pro-Boer" as epithet for dissenters** — ✅ VERIFIED. Well-documented historical term.

51. **Liberal opposition and press segments opposed the war** — ✅ VERIFIED. The Liberal Party was divided; "pro-Boer" Liberals opposed it.

52. **"Liberal landslide of 1906 was, in substantial part, a delayed referendum on the Boer War"** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. The 1906 Liberal landslide was real, but historians attribute it to multiple factors: tariff reform controversy, "Chinese slavery" in South Africa, education policy, and Boer War disillusionment. The Boer War was *one* factor, not necessarily the primary one. The essay hedges with "in substantial part."

### Algerian War / Fifth Republic
53. **Algerian War 1954-1962** — ✅ VERIFIED.

54. **"military coup attempt, de Gaulle's return to power, and ultimately the creation of the Fifth Republic"** — ✅ VERIFIED. The May 1958 crisis, triggered by the Algerian War, brought de Gaulle back and led to the Fifth Republic's constitution.

55. **Fifth Republic as "a more centralized, executive-heavy constitutional order designed precisely to insulate presidential authority from legislative and popular constraint"** — ✅ VERIFIED. This is the standard characterization of the Fifth Republic's design.

### Lancet Studies
56. **"Lancet studies that estimated 600,000+ excess deaths were published in 2006"** — ⚠️ PARTIALLY CORRECT. The second Lancet study (October 2006) estimated **654,965 excess deaths** (with 601,000 violent). "600,000+" is a reasonable approximation but understates the figure. The essay should say "~655,000" for accuracy.

## Critical Errors Requiring Correction

### ❌ Must Fix
1. **Korea War initial approval: 66% → should be 78%** (Section IV). Gallup clearly shows 78% approved Truman's decision. This is the most significant factual error in the essay.

2. **Arendt quote in Section VII** ("the most dangerous moment for a society is not when it is confronted by evil but when it loses the capacity to recognize evil as evil — when the monstrous becomes normal"). This appears to be a **fabricated quote** attributed to *Origins of Totalitarianism*. While it captures Arendtian themes, I cannot find this text in any verified collection of Arendt's writings. The essay states "Arendt wrote in *The Origins of Totalitarianism*" — this is a direct attribution claim. **Recommend removing the direct attribution or replacing with a verified quote.**

### ⚠️ Should Fix
3. **Soleimani "without congressional notification"** → "without prior congressional consultation" (he did notify within 48 hours per War Powers Act).
4. **Lancet "600,000+"** → "~655,000" for precision.
5. **Brothers of Italy lineage** — add intermediate steps (AN, PdL) or soften to "traces its lineage to."
6. **Sicilian Expedition "destroyed Athenian democracy"** — overstates; weakened Athens but democracy persisted another decade.
7. **1906 Liberal landslide as Boer War referendum** — multiple causes; consider softening.
8. **Iraq "fell below 40% by 2005"** — support was more like 40-47%; "fell below majority" would be more accurate.
9. **Vietnam "28% by 1971"** — specific figure not readily confirmed; consider "below 30%."

## Overall Assessment

The essay is **well-researched and largely accurate** on its historical claims. The two significant errors are:
1. A wrong number (Korea 66% vs actual 78%)
2. A likely fabricated Arendt quote

The remaining issues are imprecisions, oversimplifications, and paraphrases presented slightly too close to direct quotation. For an academic roundtable paper, the Korea figure and the Arendt attribution **must be corrected** before publication. The other items are judgment calls about precision vs. readability.

**Reliability score: 88/100** — Strong but not publication-ready without the two critical corrections.

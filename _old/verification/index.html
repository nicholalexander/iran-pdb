Note on AI-Generated Historical Writing

Where AI is reliable: Dates, names, well-documented events, structural arguments, source citations (book/chapter numbers were consistently accurate), and bibliographic references (all cited secondary sources are real published works).

Where AI is unreliable: Specific statistics and numbers (most common error type), direct quotations (tendency to produce paraphrases as quotes), attribution of quotes to specific speakers, and literary quotations (highest risk for fabrication).

Recommendation: AI-generated historical writing should always undergo human fact-checking, with particular scrutiny of direct quotations, specific numbers, and any literary or poetic quotations.

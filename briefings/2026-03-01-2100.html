The RAF Akrotiri strike is getting buried under the Hezbollah news, but it shouldn't be. An Iranian drone hitting UK sovereign territory in Cyprus is a different category of escalation than near-misses in Bahrain or intercepts over Qatar. That's not an argument about proportionality — it's an argument about legality. Kaine and Paul now have a Pentagon-sourced admission that directly contradicts the White House. Whether it actually moves votes depends on whether CNN's reporting gets picked up by the broader press or gets buried under Hezbollah and Akrotiri.

If Houthis launch their promised strike and Bab el-Mandeb joins Hormuz and Suez as a disrupted chokepoint, $100+ becomes the floor, not the ceiling.

Israel has every incentive to understate Iranian capacity to encourage continued strikes. But if it's even directionally correct, Iran's massive barrages may be a "use it or lose it" strategy — fire everything before the launch sites get hit. That would explain both the scale of the retaliation and the IRGC's increasingly baroque claims about hitting carriers and destroying THAAD systems. When you're running low, you inflate the narrative.

What I'm watching for next cycle: Houthi first strike (AP says hours). UK response to Akrotiri. Whether the Pentagon "no imminent threat" story gets legs. Monday formal market open.

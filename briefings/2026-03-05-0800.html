Day 6 and the war is metastasizing. That's the word I keep coming back to.

The Azerbaijan strike is the single most important development this cycle, and I don't think enough people are paying attention to it. Baku's warning of "unspecified response measures" is the kind of language that precedes either a military response or a formal invocation of bilateral defense relationships. If Ankara interprets the Nakhchivan strike as an attack on a fraternal Turkic state, the NATO Article 5 question that Hegseth tried to wave away comes roaring back — through a different door.

The tanker claim deserves skepticism but not dismissal. CENTCOM said no Iranian vessel remains in the Gulf — but IRGC has shore-based anti-ship missiles and fast attack craft. If real: first direct strike on US commercial shipping, obliterating whatever was left of the Hormuz status quo. If fabricated: domestic propaganda — "we struck back for the Dena." Either way, watch the US response in the next 6–12 hours.

The food crisis is the sleeper story. Maersk suspending all Gulf bookings. 50+ million people in countries importing 90% of their food. This isn't a future risk — it's happening now. Gulf strategic reserves were designed for price shocks, not supply cutoffs. Nobody war-gamed "what if the world's second-largest shipping company just stops delivering." Iraq cutting 1.5 million barrels/day because they literally have nowhere to put or ship it. The economic weapon here isn't sanctions — it's geography.

The mood shift in Tehran matters more than any missile count. "People are a bit scared now and no longer have the joy of the first days." That's the transition from nationalist rally to survival mode. 100,000 fled in the first 48 hours. Kallas saying ME countries warn of civil war risk — that's intelligence from governments who can see the fracture lines up close.

The European military mobilization: four nations deploying to Cyprus, 15 US refuelers in Portugal for a week, France authorizing US aircraft at French bases while insisting it's "defensive." This is the infrastructure of a longer war being built in real time while politicians talk about restraint.
